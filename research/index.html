Job Market Paper:
The Individual Level Antecedents to Corporate Political Stances
Extended Abstract
  • Research Question: What factors lead executives to take political stances as part of a business strategy?

  • Key Finding: Monetary, behavioral, and institutional forces causally impact these decisions, with differential effects based on the executive's political and ethical orientations.

    The Cases for Diversity in Promotions (with Sarah Kaplan and András Tilcsik)
    Extended Abstract (To Be Added)
    • Research Question: How do different types of executive messaging for diversity and inclusion impact managerial decisions to promote under-represented minorities?

    • Key Finding: Managers promote minority candidates more often when executives message diversity using a social justice oriented "moral case" compared to a more instrumental "business case".

    The Impact of Highly Publicized Campus Scandals on College Outcomes (with Jonathan Smith) in Contemporary Economic Policy 37(3):492-508.
    • Research Question: Do highly publicized scandals have an impact on outcomes at selective U.S. universities?

    • Key Finding: Schools with highly publicized scandals witness a 10% decrease in applications the following year, roughly the impact of dropping 10 spots in prominent college rankings.
